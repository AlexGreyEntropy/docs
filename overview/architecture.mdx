A Thaler vault is composed of four parts. Each part has a single, well-defined responsibility. The boundaries between them are enforced on chain so that no party, including the protocol, can bypass them.

## The four parts

<Steps>
  <Step title="The Squads smart account">
    Every vault is an instance of a Squads smart account on Solana. The account holds the deposit
    and is the only address that can sign on the vault's behalf. Users retain co-ownership of the
    account at all times.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The policy extension">
    A Squads policy extension is co-signed by the user at creation. It enumerates the allowed
    assets, the allowed venues, the leverage cap, the claim rules and the closure procedure. After
    the user signs it once, the policy is immutable. The smart account refuses any instruction
    that falls outside the policy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The dedicated worker">
    A Cloudflare Worker instance is provisioned per vault. It watches market conditions and emits
    instructions that match the policy. The worker cannot widen the policy, sign on behalf of any
    other vault, or settle assets to an address that the policy has not authorised.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The agent wallet">
    A Privy agent wallet is used by the worker to co-sign permitted instructions. Its signing
    authority is scoped, time-bounded, and audited on every action. It cannot move funds to an
    arbitrary address; the smart account's policy extension is the authority that decides what is
    valid.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How the parts coordinate

A vault always operates within the same loop:

1. The worker reads a market signal (a staking yield update, a borrow rate change, a funding rate flip).
2. It composes an instruction that the policy permits.
3. The agent wallet co-signs the instruction with the smart account.
4. The Solana network executes it on chain.
5. The change of state is recorded by the protocol and shown to the user.

If any step fails the policy check, the network rejects the transaction. There is no off-chain override.

## Why this structure

The structure separates **custody** (Squads smart account) from **rules** (policy extension), from **execution** (Cloudflare Worker), from **signing** (Privy agent wallet). Each can be reviewed independently and each can be replaced without touching the others.

A user who wants to verify any vault can read its policy on chain and confirm that the worker is operating inside it. There is no privileged path.

## What the user signs

At creation, the user signs:

- The Squads policy extension. This is the rule set the vault will follow for its entire life.
- The Terms and Policy Agreement. This is the legal acknowledgement that the user understands the non-discretionary execution model and the residual risks of the underlying venues.

After creation, the only on-vault signatures the user produces are for the `claim` and `close` operations. Everything else happens inside the policy bounds described above.
